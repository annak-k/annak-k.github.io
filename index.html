anna kapron-king
I am a first year PhD student in Natural Language Processing at the University of Edinburgh, supervised by Simon Kirby and Kenny Smith. My interests include computational models of pragmatics and language evolution, and more generally, using computational methods to develop a better understanding of human language and cognition.

I received an MSc in Computer Science from the University of Toronto, supervised by Yang Xu. I used computational methods to explore euphemism change, including analyzing gender differences in euphemism use using online diachronic corpora. Prior to that, I completed an MSc in Linguistics at the University of Edinburgh.
